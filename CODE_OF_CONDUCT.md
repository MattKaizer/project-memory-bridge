# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project wants direct, respectful, technically honest collaboration.

We welcome contributions, questions, and disagreement when they help the work improve. What matters is the quality of the reasoning and the respect shown to other people.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Critique ideas, code, and decisions — not people.
- Explain technical disagreement with evidence.
- Assume good intent, but do not skip rigor.
- Keep discussions focused on helping the project improve.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Discrimination or exclusionary behavior
- Deliberate bad-faith disruption
- Repeatedly ignoring project boundaries or maintainer direction
- Publishing private information without permission

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, or contributions that violate this code of conduct.

In serious or repeated cases, maintainers may block further participation.

## Reporting

If something crosses the line, contact the maintainers through the repository's issue or discussion channels, or use a private maintainer contact path if one is available.

## Practical interpretation

Strong technical feedback is welcome.

Being blunt is not the same as being abusive. But clarity is never an excuse for contempt. We are here to build better software, not worse conversations.
